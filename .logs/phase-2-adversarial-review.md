# Phase 2 adversarial review

Date: 2026-07-17
Scope: Phase 2 narrative-system sample only

## Verdict

The structure is on track. The main risk is a mechanics showcase with dialogue attached. This gate should prove one tense room problem that happens to exercise the systems. It should not feel like a tour of claims, saves, silence, schedules, and provenance.

The 500-path simulation proves deterministic safety and reachability. It does not prove that the scene is fun, tense, or worth replaying. Those questions still require human playtests.

## Risks and safeguards

| Risk                                         | Required safeguard                                                                                                                           |
| -------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Dialogue becomes the whole game              | At least one service or tuning action must change a later line, an object, and a social response.                                            |
| Attention becomes an abstract branch menu    | Every offer must arise from the room. One is heard directly, one leaves a fragment, and one is genuinely missed.                             |
| Claims become collectibles                   | Show attribution and provenance only. Do not show truth colours, completion counts, or a branch map.                                         |
| Silence becomes the correct patience button  | The sample silences must differ. One invites speech, one permits interruption, and one reads as agreement or avoidance.                      |
| Determinism becomes an obvious optimal route | Keep arithmetic hidden and context-dependent. Reproducible results must not advertise one correct play style.                                |
| Characters become dossiers                   | Do not reveal every contradiction. Each substantial patron needs a relationship motive outside political position.                           |
| Broadcasts become history lessons            | A public event must interrupt work, change posture or an object, and unsettle a prior claim.                                                 |
| Replay becomes branch completion             | Two routes should reach the same public result but leave different defensible readings of a patron. Neither route should recover everything. |
| Save and load encourage branch mining        | Preserve exact state without showing future branches or missed-content counts.                                                               |
| Sasha and Galina support male exposition     | A material choice by Sasha or Galina must change who can speak, what stays private, or which event is heard.                                 |

## Phase 2 product gate

A tester should be able to name:

- the practical room problem they handled;
- what they knowingly chose to miss;
- one patron as a person rather than an ideology;
- a material consequence of service, tuning, or silence;
- how a second route changed their interpretation without changing public history.

The reason to retry should be unresolved human curiosity, not a missing-content counter.

## Later steering questions

- Should missed scenes stay sharply absent, or should the room usually provide a biased second-hand fragment? Current recommendation: preserve at least one fully missed event.
- Should Sasha keep a sparse visible claim ledger, or should provenance remain mostly implicit in dialogue wording? Current recommendation: a sparse attributed ledger with no truth or completion markers.
- Should the opening lean toward comic status games or immediate intimate pressure?
- Should Sasha read first as a sharp observer or a practical custodian?

These questions do not block the Phase 2 systems gate. They should be answered before production voice and final content are locked.
