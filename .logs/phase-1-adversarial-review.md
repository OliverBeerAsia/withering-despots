# Phase 1 adversarial review

Date: 2026-07-16

## Verdict

The project is structurally on track. The fixed chamber, bartender verbs, and scarce attention form a clear identity. Phase 1 proves that the grammar is operable and readable. It does not yet prove that the full game will be fun, emotionally durable, or worth replaying.

## What is working

- Attention is the central resource. Choosing the television means missing the telephone, and the lost channel is visible.
- The bartender verbs suit the setting. Observe, Speak, Serve, Tune, and Wait keep the player inside Sasha's role.
- The room behaves like a proscenium. People, service, media, and silence compete within one readable composition.
- Waiting is a real action, not a failure to find the correct hotspot.
- The player cannot fix history. The meaningful question is what they notice, encourage, interrupt, and remember.

## Corrections made during review

- Moved television and telephone choices into the room and removed menu-like attention controls.
- Removed hidden sixth verbs from the HUD.
- Added visible state consequences so choices do not disappear into text alone.
- Fixed room geometry, routes, draw order, focus behavior, interface scaling, modal input, and unstable PixiJS redraws.
- Removed player-facing development and process language outside the deliberate collapsed diagnostics control.
- Added durable writing guidance to `AGENTS.md`: direct human language, no em dashes, no generic synthetic filler, and no self-referential process copy in the game.

## Risks for later phases

- A ten-action proof can become a linear checklist once dialogue arrives. Later scenes need overlapping motives and imperfect information, not a sequence of content buttons.
- The five verbs can become cosmetic if each maps to one obvious result. Their value must depend on timing, person, context, and what Sasha has already witnessed.
- A static room can lose energy. Blocking, eyelines, interrupted rituals, and changing clusters must carry tension without turning into visual noise.
- Broadcasts can become exposition dispensers. They should alter conduct in the room, not merely deliver facts.
- Patron contradictions must appear through behavior and pressure. Biography summaries will flatten them into ideological representatives.
- Silence needs authored consequences that are sometimes generous, sometimes evasive, and sometimes costly.
- Replay value should come from missed testimony, changed trust, and different interpretations, not from collecting every branch.

## Disposable scaffolding

Tea increasing composure and vodka increasing intoxication exist only to prove deterministic service effects. They are not approved production meaning. The final service model must avoid moral shorthand and treat serving as social conduct shaped by person, moment, and intent.

## Tests to run when authored content begins

- Can a first-time player explain what they chose to miss and why?
- Does waiting ever feel charged rather than empty?
- Can two players leave with different but defensible readings of the same patron?
- Does serving change the social temperature rather than act as a stat button?
- Can the opening create a memorable human hook before the historical situation is explained?
- Does the room remain readable when two conversations and one broadcast compete?

## User steering needed before later production

1. Should the opening lead with comic status games that slowly expose pain, or intimate eavesdropping under immediate pressure?
2. Should playing Sasha feel primarily like being a sharp social observer, or a practical custodian trying to hold a room together?

The visual recommendation for later exploration is a service-worker proscenium crossed by a television-to-telephone signal diagonal. It gives Sasha's practical work and the attention conflict equal compositional weight.
