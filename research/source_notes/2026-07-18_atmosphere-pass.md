# Atmosphere pass source note

Date: 2026-07-18

## Purpose and scope

This note supports a slower evening in the bar through service labor, competing broadcasts, quiet television states, weather traces, and small acts of routine. It does not establish a generic Soviet atmosphere or a playlist of familiar Russian songs.

The working establishment is a modest second-category cafe-bar. That is a plausible composite used to interpret period regulations. The regulations document practices available to establishments in a class. They do not prove that one real Moscow venue combined every practice below.

## Service practices

The USSR Ministry of Trade classification order supports bar-counter service by a bartender or buffet worker, recorded music through radio equipment, and a printed menu displayed in a special frame. These findings map to SERVICE-001 through SERVICE-003.

Source: [Order No. 214 on Classification of Public Catering Enterprises](https://normativ.kontur.ru/document?documentId=265674&moduleId=1), sections 2.2.3 and 2.4.4.

The 1974 basic rules support placing accepted receipts or meal tickets on spikes for end-of-shift counting, providing an ashtray on request in waiter-served establishments, and a limited cleaning break for halls operating at least ten hours. These findings map to SERVICE-004, SERVICE-005, and SERVICE-008.

Source: [Order No. 147 on Basic Rules for Public Catering Enterprises](https://meganorm.ru/mega_doc/dop_norm/0/metodicheskie_ukazaniya_po_organizatsii_ratsionalnogo/0/prikaz_mintorga_sssr_ot_09_07_1974_N_147_ob_utverzhdenii.html), sections 1.6, 3.5, and 5.2.

The 1980 classification order includes telephone service for visitors. It does not establish that the telephone sat behind the counter or that patrons needed the bartender's permission. That interaction remains a plausible composite under SERVICE-006.

The complaints and suggestions book was a required bureaucratic object in retail and public catering establishments and had to be supplied on request. This supports SERVICE-007, but not an exact cover, layout, or set of entries.

Source: [Instruction on the Complaints and Suggestions Book in Retail and Public Catering Enterprises](https://pravo.ppt.ru/prikaz/mintorg-rsfsr/n-346-69592), sections 1 through 4.

## Broadcast chronology

Source: [Soviet Coup Collapses: Gorbachev Returns, in Full Control](https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1991-08-22-mn-1188-story.html), contemporary dispatch on resumed Russian radio and television.

The evening Vremya bulletin on 21 August visibly reversed course and reported the restoration of Gorbachev's authority. This supports a scheduled 21:00 change in subject and presenter tone under MEDIA-004. The game should use fictional wording and original visuals.

Source: [El principal informativo anunció la recuperación del poder por Gorbachov](https://elpais.com/diario/1991/08/22/internacional/682812021_850215.html), published 22 August 1991.

Ekho Moskvy was shut down again at 08:00 on 21 August and later restored through a reserve transmitter. Correspondents supplied reports through ordinary telephone calls. This supports interruptions, uncertain reception, and phone-fed reports under MEDIA-005.

Source: [Radiopriemniki na barrikadakh](https://yeltsin.ru/news/polnaya-versiya-radiopriemniki-na-barrikadah/), passage on the 21 August shutdown and reserve transmitter.

Participants in the emergency session described the lack of objective information and reliance on receivers for Radio Liberty, the BBC, and CNN. This supports a design in which sources conflict and reception remains fragmentary. It does not guarantee that every service was clearly audible inside the fictional bar. This maps to MEDIA-006.

The same primary transcript describes defenders outside the Russian parliament in rain. It supports wet coats, umbrella drips, and damp traces, but not continuous rain throughout the playable night. This maps to EVENT-003.

Source: [Transcript of the First Extraordinary Session of the Supreme Soviet of the Russian Federation](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/media/28349/ocr), translated excerpts on pages 2 through 4 and page 37.

Gorbachev reached Vnukovo Airport shortly after 02:00 on 22 August. This supports a scheduled arrival report under EVENT-004. It does not by itself prove exact live television camerawork.

Source: [Gorbachev returns to Moscow](https://www.upi.com/Archives/1991/08/21/Gorbachev-returns-to-Moscow/7971682747200/), contemporary arrival dispatch.

## Mechanical and material ambience

The public-catering sanitary rules approved by the USSR Ministries of Health and Trade in March 1991 required ventilation in production rooms. They also treated an establishment's refrigeration equipment as part of its operating equipment. This supports a low service-zone air or duct bed, but not a conspicuous fan in the patron hall or a particular mechanical signature. This maps to FACILITY-001.

Source: [SanPiN 42-123-5777-91, Sanitary Rules for Public Catering Enterprises](https://meganorm.ru/mega_doc/norm/normy/6/sanpin_42-123-5777-91_sanitarnye_pravila_dlya_predpriyatiya.html), sections 1.2, 5.2, and 7.1 through 7.4.

The Soviet design standard for public-catering enterprises required separate electrical supply lines for refrigeration installations where used. This makes refrigeration equipment plausible in the service zone. It does not establish an under-counter refrigerator, its model, its placement, whether its compressor was audible in the hall, or a regular operating cycle. Any compressor sound remains a restrained fictional composite under FACILITY-002.

Source: [SNiP II-L.8-71, Public Catering Enterprises Design Standards](https://meganorm.ru/mega_doc/norm/normy/4/snip_II-l_8-71_chast_II_razdel_l_glava_8_predpriyatiya.html), section 5.2.

The 1980 classification order lists two-seat and four-seat tables, chairs, and bar stools for a second-category bar. It also specifies blown glassware as an allowed tableware class. These findings support ordinary chair shifts and restrained glass placement sounds. They do not verify the current chair or faceted-glass sprite geometry, material, wear, dimensions, or exact acoustic character. These findings map to FURNITURE-001 and TABLEWARE-001, while PROP-001 and PROP-002 remain unverified for exact visual form.

Source: [Order No. 214 on Classification of Public Catering Enterprises](https://normativ.kontur.ru/document?documentId=265674&moduleId=1), section 2.4.4.

## Implementation guidance

Keep the television on for long quiet stretches. Its sound can sit low or nearly muted until a scheduled bulletin or a patron's choice brings it forward. The radio should be a separate source with its own signal quality and interruptions.

Useful grounded actions include placing receipts on a spike, supplying an ashtray only after a request, checking the framed menu, asking to use the telephone, wiping wet marks near the entrance, and beginning a conditional pre-dawn cleaning lull. These actions create duration without inventing new plot machinery.

During a long quiet beat, use the room in layers: continuous low air and receiver presence, a conditional refrigeration start or stop from the service zone, then one motivated chair, glass, paper, or telephone action. Avoid firing every layer at once or on a mechanically obvious loop. A receiver can be lifted, held, returned, tuned, or listened to, but the exact telephone body, bell cadence, radio model, and switch sounds remain unverified.

## Limits and cautions

- Do not run Swan Lake continuously through the evening of 21 August.
- Do not use Korobeiniki, Kalinka, or Ochi Chornye as unexamined ambient shorthand. MUSIC-002 remains unverified.
- Do not give the television modern continuous-news behavior.
- Do not assert exact electronics, ashtray, telephone, tea-glass holder, or glassware models without dated visual sources. PROP-002 remains unverified.
- Do not turn ventilation or refrigeration into loud industrial ambience. The sources establish systems and equipment classes, not a constant audible drone in the customer room.
- Do not treat a chair scrape, glass clink, or telephone bell as a period marker by itself. Keep each action socially motivated and acoustically restrained.
- Do not make rain continuous across every game hour.
- Do not reproduce archival broadcast wording, station recordings, or presenter likenesses.

The official regulations are primary sources for service rules. The legislative transcript is a primary source for statements made during the session. Contemporary journalism and the museum account are secondary evidence for chronology and broadcast behavior. Exact local reception, decor, models, repertoire, and the fictional venue's combined staffing arrangement remain constrained or unverified.
